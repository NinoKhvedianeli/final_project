Popular Destinations

Tours give you the opportunity to see different regions of Georgia

Svaneti

Svaneti, a historic province in northwestern Georgia, is renowned for its mountainous terrain along the Greater Caucasus range. It features the highest inhabited area in the Caucasus, with peaks like Mount Shkhara. The region is divided into Upper and Lower Svaneti, known for their deep gorges and diverse landscapes ranging from mixed forests to alpine meadows. With a rich history, Svaneti has preserved its unique culture, including the Svan language and traditional tower houses. The UNESCO-listed village of Ushguli and the region's distinct polyphonic singing highlight Svaneti's cultural significance and picturesque beauty.

Adjara

Adjara is a region in southwestern Georgia, bordered by the Black Sea and Turkey. Its capital, Batumi, is Georgia's second most populous city. Adjara, home to around 350,000 people, features diverse landscapes from coastal lowlands to the Lesser Caucasus Mountains. Historically influenced by various empires, including the Ottoman and Russian Empires, Adjara retains a unique cultural identity. Known for its humid climate and lush forests, the region is a significant tourist destination. The region's political status and autonomy are constitutionally guaranteed, with governance provided by the Supreme Council and the President-nominated local government.

Kakheti

Kakheti, located in eastern Georgia, is renowned as one of the country's foremost wine-producing regions, celebrated for its traditional winemaking methods and diverse varietals. Home to a variety of Georgian wines, Kakheti benefits from a favorable climate and fertile lands that have supported viticulture for millennia. The region's winemaking heritage is deeply embedded in its cultural fabric, drawing visitors to its vineyards, wine cellars, and wine festivals throughout the year. Kakheti's significance in Georgia's wine industry underscores its role as a cultural and economic hub, contributing to its rich historical legacy and modern-day appeal as a top tourist destination.
